## Live QA (owner, on his machine, dev build post wave 2)

1. **Nav redesign (#36/#138)** — ✅ "yes all good": labels, lightbulb Coach,
   gear Settings, curved active indicator, title→Dashboard, <640px icon-only
   collapse, keyboard activation.
2. **Analysis effect sentences (#17/#132)** — ✅ "looks good". Observations
   from this pass:
   - Caffeine rows showed no correlations — expected, not a defect: the
     engine requires ≥14 days with both the predictor and outcome recorded
     (`min_days=14`) plus variance in both columns (ADR 003: analysis only
     uses explicitly recorded days).
   - Minute-unit bin averages read as raw minutes ("avg 446.9") — owner
     directed the duration idiom instead; fixed same-day as #147 ("7h 27m",
     shipped in this release).
3. **Evening-clock normalization (#134/#142)** — ⏭️ deliberately skipped by
   the owner ("don't feel like testing this, maybe someday in the future").
   Risk accepted: behavior is pinned by regression tests (per-column
   after-midnight semantics, the 4 AM boundary, an end-to-end correlation
   sign-flip, and the early-riser-loses-to-afternoon aggregation case).
4. **Dashboard pills (#37/#141)** — ✅ "all looks good, nice job on the
   window resizing": wording, colors, no overflow at half-width windows.
5. **Monthly best/worst night line (#113/#139)** — ✅ line verified against
   the Oura app (bed time, duration, deep/REM, HRV render correctly). The
   cross-check surfaced two findings:
   - Best-night selection breaks score ties arbitrarily (`max` returns the
     first 91 of two), picking a substantively weaker night — tie-break fix
     proposed, awaiting the owner's call (ride the release or v0.1.2).
   - Oura's daily view credits naps; Somnus stores only the main sleep
     period — recorded as concrete evidence on #115 (naps from Oura).
6. **Onboarding/Settings target hints (#38/#141)** — ✅ "looks good": fields
   say what each target powers (bedtime → countdown/caffeine
   marker/consistency; wake → Auto display mode); both marked optional.
7. **Caffeine chart labels (#106/#135)** — ✅ "looks good" (axis labels);
   the after-midnight bedtime-marker scenario deliberately skipped
   ("didn't feel like messing with the late caffeine testing") — risk
   accepted, pinned by the #135 wrapBedtimeHour regression tests.
8. **Weekly Top Factors (#102/#125)** — ✅ verified working: all-time
   caption renders ("Computed across all 361 days of your data — not
   specific to this week"). Owner initially read "top-3 per direction" as
   a promise of three items; actual behavior is *up to* 3 past the
   |r| ≥ 0.1 noise floor — his data currently qualifies one factor
   (Bedtime, r=-0.32) on the "worse" side and none on the "better" side,
   whose empty section is omitted rather than padded. Explained; QA-item
   wording, not a defect.
9. **Venv setup (#105/#133)** — ⏭️ skipped by the owner ("skipping for
   now"); covered by the CI value-test added in #133.
